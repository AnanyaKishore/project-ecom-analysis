E-commerce Sales Analysis

Overview
  • This project analyzes sales patterns and customer behaviour from Brazilian e-commerce data collected between September 2016 and September 2018.
  • The datasets have been collected from here.
  • Uses Pandas for data cleaning, storage and wrangling.
  • Uses Plotly for visualization.

To view the master repository, see here.

Data Cleaning
  • Exploratory Data Analysis performed for all three datasets to analyze number of records, relationships between the datasets and to extract relevant information.
  • Removing records from the customer orders dataset that do not have a legitimate order_id.
  • Merging the three datasets into a master dataset.
  • Efficient conversion of columns to suitable datatypes.
  • For efficient storage for further analysis, the master dataset is stored as a parquet file.
  • This preserves the datatypes and the data is read in ~2 times faster than the CSV counterpart, while also taking up only half the storage size of the latter.
  • Removing records where the order timeline is nonsensical (shipped before approved, delivered before shipped, and so on).
  • Imputation done for missing date information wherever necessary.

For a detailed analysis of the cleaning process, see here.

Data Visualization
  • Relevant KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) calculated from the cleaned master dataset.
  • Five categories of plots created, namely: Orders, Customers, State-Wise, Time-Based and Trends.
  • To view and analyze these plots, jump to the desired tab by clicking on its name.
  • Hover over any section of each plot to see the necessary statistics.

For a detailed analysis of the visualization process, see here.

Most Frequently Observed: 1 Day
On average, an order is shipped 2.83 days after it has been purchased.
On average, shipping to Amazonas is the fastest (2.30 days), supported by its capital Manaus' role as a major hub with extensive river transport networks. Shipping to Roraima takes the longest (4.72 days), as Boa Vista has limited infrastructure and is isolated, relying on slower road routes.

Static Analysis
In the early phase of e-commerce adoption, low order volumes and limited operational maturity meant delivery speed was not yet a priority. As a result, in September 2016, the average delivery time was nearly two months. With wider adoption and improved logistics, delivery times dropped sharply, reaching a low of 4 days in December 2016, despite the holiday-season surge. In the following months, delivery times stabilized between 7 and 14 days.
Early order volumes were low, but as e-commerce expanded, monthly orders rose to 4,000–5,000 during May-October 2017. November saw a peak of 8,664 orders, followed by 6,302 in December, driven by the holiday season. Thereafter, volumes stabilized between 6,500 and 8,000 per month.
